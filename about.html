about this archive

✻

This collection takes its name from Lord Byron's "Darkness," a poem composed during the "year without a summer" of 1816. The line "and men were faithful to a corpse" speaks to our tenuous relationship with memory, tradition, and the past.

The writings collected here explore themes of impermanence, digital preservation, and the search for meaning in fragmentary knowledge.

"And men forgot their passions in the dread
Of this their desolation; and all hearts
Were chill'd into a selfish prayer for light..."

The site's design embraces minimalism not merely as an aesthetic choice, but as a philosophical position—an acknowledgment that clarity emerges from reduction rather than accumulation.

All content is maintained through GitHub Pages, allowing for version control and transparent revision history—a modern approach to preserving thought against digital entropy.
