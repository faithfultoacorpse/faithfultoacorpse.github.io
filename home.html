In a time when "men were not alone," yet "knew not what to do," I collect thoughts among darkness visible.

Here lie writings and reflections—gleaned from the twilight hours, when memory and imagination converge.

"The bright sun was extinguish'd, and the stars
Did wander darkling in the eternal space,
Rayless, and pathless, and the icy earth
Swung blind and blackening in the moonless air..."
— Lord Byron, Darkness
